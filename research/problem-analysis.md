# Problem Analysis

## Background

Agriculture is the backbone of India's economy, but many farmers suffer crop losses because plant diseases are detected too late. In rural areas, access to agricultural experts is limited, and internet connectivity is often unreliable.

## Problem

Farmers face difficulties in:
- Identifying crop diseases at an early stage.
- Choosing the correct treatment.
- Accessing expert guidance in remote areas.
- Understanding sustainable farming practices.

As a result, excessive use of chemical pesticides increases farming costs, damages soil health, and negatively impacts the environment.

## Proposed Solution

**KrishiMitra AI** is an AI-powered crop disease detection system that allows farmers to capture a crop image using a smartphone. The system analyzes the image and provides:

- 🌿 **Disease Detection Score (Confidence %)** – Shows how confident the AI is about its prediction.

- 📊 **Disease Severity Score** – Indicates how serious the infection is.

- 🧭 **Disease Stage** – Classifies the disease as Early, Moderate, or Severe.

- 📖 **Disease Information** – Provides details about the disease, its symptoms, causes, and affected crops.

- 🗣️ **Marathi Advisory** – Explains the disease and recommendations in simple Marathi.

- 🌱 **Organic Treatment Recommendations (Priority)** – Suggests eco-friendly and sustainable solutions before chemical treatments.

- 🛡️ **Safe Chemical Recommendations (Only if Necessary)** – Recommends scientifically approved chemical treatments only for severe cases.

- 📋 **Prevention Tips** – Provides preventive measures to reduce future disease outbreaks.

- 📍 **Nearest Krishi Kendra Information** – Helps farmers locate the nearest agriculture service center for expert support.

### Our Goal

The goal of KrishiMitra AI is not only to detect crop diseases but also to empower farmers with sustainable farming practices, reduce unnecessary chemical pesticide usage, improve crop productivity, and promote environmentally responsible agriculture.

## Expected Impact

- Early disease detection
- Reduced crop loss
- Lower farming costs
- Reduced unnecessary pesticide use
- Promotion of sustainable agriculture
- Improved farmer awareness and productivity
